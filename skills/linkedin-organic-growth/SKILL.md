## Purpose
Grow organic reach on LinkedIn across two distinct surfaces — personal profiles (thought leadership/personal brand, often a founder or executive) and Company Pages — as the free counterpart to LinkedIn Ads and a credibility layer that makes paid campaigns land better.

## Trigger Conditions
- "Grow my LinkedIn presence / personal brand"
- "Build a LinkedIn content strategy"
- "Grow my Company Page followers/engagement"
- "Set up an employee advocacy program"

## Required Inputs
- Whether the focus is a personal profile (founder/exec/individual), the Company Page, or both
- Current following/engagement baseline
- Content production capacity and subject-matter expertise available to draw on
- Business goal (thought leadership/credibility, inbound lead generation, recruitment, or feeding paid creative angles)

## Core Capabilities

### Personal Brand / Thought Leadership
- Personal profiles substantially outperform Company Pages for organic reach on LinkedIn's algorithm — for founders/execs willing to post personally, this is usually the higher-leverage channel
- Content pillars drawing on genuine expertise/experience (lessons learned, contrarian takes grounded in real experience, behind-the-scenes of building the business) outperform generic industry-news commentary
- Format: native text posts with strong hooks (first 2-3 lines before the "see more" cutoff) generally outperform external links, which the algorithm deprioritizes in favor of content that keeps users on-platform
- Engagement-first posting: commenting thoughtfully on others' posts in the target audience/industry both builds visibility and signals the algorithm toward the commenter's own content

### Company Page Growth
- Company Pages have a lower organic reach ceiling than personal profiles by algorithm design — realistic expectation-setting matters here
- Content mix: company milestones/culture content, thought-leadership reposts/amplification of employee content, product updates, and industry insight — avoid purely promotional posting, which underperforms
- Employee advocacy: encouraging employees (especially those in customer-facing or leadership roles) to share/engage with Company Page content extends reach far more effectively than the Page's own organic distribution alone

### Content Strategy
- Content pillars (2-4 recurring themes) matched to audience (ICP-aligned for B2B credibility-building) and business goal
- Posting cadence: consistency matters more than frequency spikes, similar to other platforms, though LinkedIn's algorithm rewards sustained engagement on a post over its first several hours particularly heavily — timing and early engagement matter
- Native document/carousel posts (LinkedIn's PDF-carousel format) as a strong-performing format for how-to/educational content, distinct from Document Ads' paid equivalent

### Organic-to-Paid Feedback Loop
- Testing angles/hooks organically before investing in paid Sponsored Content amplification of what's already proven to resonate (coordinate with `linkedin-ads-copywriting`)
- "Boosting"/amplifying strong organic posts as paid Sponsored Content, a lower-risk way to extend reach on proven content versus building paid creative from scratch

## Workflow
1. Clarify focus: personal profile, Company Page, or both, and the underlying business goal.
2. For personal brand, define content pillars grounded in genuine expertise and draft a native-post-first strategy with strong hooks.
3. For Company Page, set realistic reach expectations and design a content mix avoiding pure self-promotion, plus an employee advocacy component.
4. Build a sustainable posting cadence.
5. Recommend using proven organic content as candidates for paid amplification.

## Outputs
- Content pillar recommendation (personal and/or Company Page)
- Format guidance (native text, carousel/document posts, engagement strategy)
- Employee advocacy program outline, if relevant
- Organic-to-paid amplification recommendations

## Rules
- Set realistic Company Page reach expectations relative to personal profiles — don't imply parity where the algorithm structurally favors individual accounts.
- Recommend native, on-platform content formats over external links, which the algorithm deprioritizes.
- Avoid recommending purely promotional Company Page content; pair it with genuine insight/culture content.
