## Purpose
Set up Spark Ads — TikTok's format for boosting an existing organic post (brand-owned or creator-authorized) as a paid ad while the post retains its native profile, engagement history, and comment section, rather than running as a disconnected ad unit.

## Trigger Conditions
- "Set up Spark Ads for my TikTok video"
- "Boost a creator's post as an ad"
- "Should I use Spark Ads or standard in-feed ads"
- "Run ads from an organic post that's performing well"

## Required Inputs
- The organic post to boost (brand's own, or a creator's — requires creator authorization via TikTok's Spark Ads Authorization Code for non-owned content)
- Campaign objective and budget this boosted post will run under
- Whether the post has already shown organic traction (a common and effective trigger for choosing Spark Ads) or is being launched fresh as a Spark Ad from day one

## Core Capabilities

### Why Spark Ads Over Standard In-Feed
- Spark Ads retain the original post's likes, comments, shares, and follower growth — engagement earned through paid promotion continues building the account's real social proof, unlike a standard in-feed ad that generates disconnected engagement metrics
- Native profile visibility (clicking the ad can lead to the actual profile) builds account following alongside campaign performance, a compounding benefit standard ads don't offer
- Often outperforms standard in-feed ads on trust/authenticity signals since viewers can see genuine comment activity and profile history

### Creator Content Authorization
- Using a creator's organic post requires their authorization via TikTok's Spark Ads Authorization Code (generated by the creator, provided to the advertiser) — this is a hard prerequisite, not optional
- Clarifying usage rights and compensation terms with creators upfront (route to `tiktok-ads-creative-strategy` for the broader creator partnership relationship)
- Authorization duration/expiry management for ongoing campaigns using creator content

### Selecting Content to Boost
- Prioritizing organic posts that have already shown above-average engagement (a natural signal of what resonates before committing paid budget) — coordinate with `tiktok-shop-organic` for organic performance data
- Fresh Spark Ads (posted with paid promotion in mind from the start, not previously organic) as a valid alternative when no proven organic content exists yet, using the same native-creative principles from `tiktok-ads-creative-strategy`

### Campaign Setup
- Spark Ads function within standard campaign objectives (Traffic, Conversions, App Promotion, Product Sales) — the format choice doesn't restrict objective selection
- Combining Spark Ads with standard in-feed ads within the same campaign for a broader creative mix, testing both formats against each other

## Workflow
1. Identify candidate content: proven organic posts (own or creator's) with above-average engagement, or plan a fresh post designed as a Spark Ad from the outset.
2. Secure creator authorization via Spark Ads Authorization Code if the content isn't brand-owned.
3. Set up the campaign under the appropriate objective, using the authorized post as the ad creative.
4. Track both paid campaign metrics and the compounding organic engagement/follower growth on the boosted post.
5. Consider running Spark Ads alongside standard in-feed ads to compare formats.

## Outputs
- Content selection recommendation (proven organic vs. fresh Spark-native post)
- Creator authorization checklist, if applicable
- Campaign setup under the chosen objective
- Combined paid + organic performance tracking recommendation

## Rules
- Never use a creator's content in a Spark Ad without proper authorization via the official Spark Ads Authorization Code process.
- Prioritize boosting proven organic performers when available — this is generally lower-risk than launching an unproven Spark-native post directly to paid budget.
- Track and report the organic engagement/follower-growth benefit alongside standard paid metrics, since this is Spark Ads' distinguishing value over standard in-feed ads.
