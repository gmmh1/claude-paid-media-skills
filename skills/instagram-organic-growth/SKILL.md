## Purpose
Grow an Instagram account's organic reach, engagement, and following — content strategy, format mix, discovery optimization, and community engagement — as the free counterpart to Meta paid ads and the creative testing ground that often informs paid creative angles.

## Trigger Conditions
- "Grow my Instagram account"
- "Build an Instagram content calendar"
- "Why aren't my Reels getting views"
- "Instagram growth strategy for [business]"

## Required Inputs
- Account niche/topic and current follower base
- Recent content performance if available (reach, engagement rate, saves/shares, follower growth)
- Content production capacity
- Business goal (community/brand building, direct sales via Shop, lead generation, or feeding a paid creative pipeline)

## Core Capabilities

### Format Mix & Discovery
- Reels as the primary discovery lever for reaching non-followers — the algorithm favors Reels for reach into new audiences far more than static feed posts
- Feed posts/carousels as stronger for engagement and saves among existing followers, less effective for reach to new audiences
- Stories for retention/relationship-building with existing followers rather than discovery — polls, questions, behind-the-scenes content sustaining daily touchpoints
- Balancing all three formats rather than over-indexing on one, since each plays a different funnel role (Reels: reach, Feed: engagement/saves, Stories: retention)

### Hook & Retention Mechanics
- Reels hook conventions mirror the paid Meta video principles in `meta-ads-video-reels`: first 1-3 seconds determine scroll-past, captions for sound-off viewing, native pacing over polished production
- Watch time/completion rate as the dominant Reels ranking signal, favoring content that rewards full viewing (loopable endings, payoff withheld just long enough to sustain interest without frustrating)

### Discovery Optimization
- Caption and alt-text keyword relevance for Instagram's search/SEO surfaces (Instagram increasingly functions as a search engine within its own ecosystem)
- Hashtags as a minor, largely legacy discovery signal relative to content quality/retention — a handful of specific, relevant tags outperforms large generic hashtag blocks
- Collab posts and creator partnerships for reach into adjacent audiences beyond the account's own follower base

### Content Strategy
- Content pillars (2-4 recurring themes/formats) matched to audience demand and business goal, building recognizable identity over scattered one-offs
- Balancing evergreen (educational, how-to, steady long-tail discovery) against trend-responsive content (audio trends, timely formats — faster reach, shorter lifespan)
- Posting consistency as a trust/algorithmic-favor signal more valuable than sporadic high-frequency bursts

### Engagement & Community
- Response speed and depth on comments/DMs affecting both relationship-building and, to some degree, algorithmic favor
- Instagram Shop/product tagging integration for ecommerce accounts turning organic content directly shoppable (coordinate with `meta-ads-catalog-feed` if the same catalog powers paid dynamic ads)

### Paid/Organic Feedback Loop
- Using organic Reels performance (which hooks/angles got genuine engagement) as low-cost signal for what to test next in paid Meta creative, and vice versa — this cross-pollination is one of Instagram's biggest practical advantages over platforms without a strong organic-to-paid feedback loop

## Workflow
1. Review recent content performance (reach vs. engagement rate) to diagnose whether the issue is discovery (low reach) or retention/relevance (reach fine, low engagement/saves).
2. If discovery is the issue, focus on Reels format, hook strength, and caption/search relevance.
3. If retention/relevance is the issue, focus on content pillar fit and audience-specific value.
4. Define 2-4 content pillars and a sustainable posting cadence across Reels/Feed/Stories.
5. Build a content calendar balancing evergreen and trend-responsive content.
6. Recommend using top-performing organic hooks/angles as paid creative test candidates (route to `meta-ads-video-reels`), and vice versa.

## Outputs
- Discovery vs. retention diagnosis
- Format mix recommendation (Reels/Feed/Stories balance)
- Content pillar and cadence recommendation
- Content calendar structure
- Organic-to-paid creative crossover recommendations

## Rules
- Never recommend hashtag volume/stuffing as a primary growth lever — content quality and retention dominate the algorithm far more.
- Diagnose discovery and retention/relevance problems separately since they have different fixes.
- Recommend Reels as the primary discovery format for accounts trying to reach beyond their existing follower base; recommend Feed/Stories investment once discovery is already healthy and retention/community becomes the priority.
