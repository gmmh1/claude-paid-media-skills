# LinkedIn Ads Client Services

> **Currency & scope note (last reviewed 2026-07-19):** Platform mechanics referenced here (character limits, campaign-type names, feature availability, thresholds, benchmark figures, policy specifics) reflect general practice as of the review date above and are **not guaranteed current** — ad platforms change quickly. ## Purpose
Turn LinkedIn Ads expertise into a sellable B2B service — pricing that reflects the platform's premium cost and longer sales cycles, proposals, onboarding, and scope of work suited to B2B clients who typically expect more rigor/reporting than SMB consumer-ad clients.

## Trigger Conditions
- "How do I sell LinkedIn Ads management as a service?"
- "Price my LinkedIn Ads retainer"
- "Write a proposal for a prospective LinkedIn Ads client"
- "Onboard a new LinkedIn Ads client"

## Required Inputs
- Target client profile (B2B SaaS, professional services, enterprise, agency-to-agency)
- Service scope offered (full management, ABM program, audit-only)
- Pricing model preference
- Whether the client has a target account list already, or needs one built (affects scope/pricing for ABM engagements specifically)

## Core Capabilities

### Pricing Models
- Flat monthly fee or percentage of ad spend, same general tradeoffs as other platforms, but LinkedIn's higher absolute CPCs mean percentage-of-spend can produce higher agency fees for comparatively less account complexity than an equivalent Meta account — be transparent about this in proposals to maintain trust
- ABM program pricing often justifies a premium/project-based fee structure separate from standard media management, given the strategic account-mapping and sales-alignment work involved beyond typical campaign management

### Proposal Structure
- B2B clients typically expect more rigor: clear ICP definition, expected CPL/CAC ranges grounded in realistic LinkedIn benchmarks, and sales-cycle-aware timelines for judging results
- Explicitly addressing the "LinkedIn is expensive" objection upfront with the deal-economics framing from `linkedin-ads-strategy`, rather than letting the client discover sticker shock after launch

### Scope of Work Boundaries
- Reporting cadence appropriate to B2B sales cycles — monthly or even quarterly deep reviews often matter more than the weekly cadence common on faster-cycle consumer platforms, though pacing/spend checks should still happen more frequently
- ABM-specific scope: is target account list development included, is sales-alignment coordination included, or is the engagement limited to campaign execution against a list the client provides
- Ad spend handling and exit terms, same considerations as other platform client-services skills

### Client Onboarding
- Discovery questionnaire: ICP definition, target account list (if any), typical deal size/sales cycle, existing Insight Tag/tracking status, CRM integration needs
- Access checklist: Campaign Manager access, Company Page admin (if organic is also in scope), CRM access for lead routing verification
- Expectation-setting on LinkedIn's longer sales-cycle feedback loop — pipeline results may not be visible for months even when the campaign itself is performing well by lead-gen metrics

## Workflow
1. Clarify target client profile and service scope, including whether ABM is part of the offering.
2. Recommend pricing, addressing LinkedIn's cost profile transparently.
3. Draft the proposal with realistic benchmark-grounded expectations and sales-cycle-aware timelines.
4. Draft scope-of-work boundaries, including ABM-specific inclusions if relevant.
5. Build the onboarding questionnaire and access checklist.
6. Set expectations on the pipeline feedback-loop delay before results are judged.

## Outputs
- Pricing model recommendation with cost-profile transparency built in
- Client-ready proposal draft
- Scope-of-work summary
- Onboarding questionnaire and access checklist
- Expectation-setting note on sales-cycle-aware result timelines

## Rules
- Always address LinkedIn's premium cost profile transparently in proposals rather than letting clients discover it after launch — this is the single most common source of early client friction on this platform.
- Set explicit expectations that pipeline/revenue results lag lead-gen metrics given typical B2B sales cycles; don't let a client judge the engagement on month-one pipeline numbers.
- Clarify ABM scope explicitly (target list development, sales alignment) since it's easy to underscope this relative to what a client assumes is included.

- **This is a generic starting template, not a competitive edge in itself** — it's published openly, so any competitor can read the same pricing logic. Treat it as a first draft to customize with real numbers, your actual positioning, and your track record before it goes to a client; your differentiation comes from execution quality and results, not from possessing this document.
- **Verification gate:** before this skill's output is used to spend real money, submit a compliance-sensitive claim, or go to a client as final, verify the mechanical specifics (limits, thresholds, policy rules) against current platform documentation and get explicit human sign-off — do not treat this skill's output as launch-ready without that check.
