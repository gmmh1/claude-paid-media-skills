## Purpose
Build video and Reels ad creative with the hook, pacing, and native structure that Meta's short-form, scroll-native environment demands — the format with the highest attention-capture ceiling and the highest execution bar.

## Trigger Conditions
- "Write a Reels ad script for [product]"
- "Build a video ad for Instagram/Facebook"
- "My video ad isn't holding attention — fix the hook"

## Required Inputs
- Product/service, offer, and audience awareness stage
- Available raw footage/assets, or whether this needs to be scripted for original production/UGC creator briefing
- Target placement (Reels, in-stream, Feed video) since pacing conventions differ slightly by surface

## Core Capabilities

### Hook Structure (First 1-3 Seconds)
- The scroll-past decision happens almost instantly — the opening frame and first line of dialogue/text must create a pattern interrupt or immediate relevance signal, not a slow brand-intro build
- Effective hook patterns: bold claim/question, visual pattern interrupt, "before" state of a before/after, direct address to the specific audience ("if you have [problem]...")
- Avoid opening with logo/brand slates or slow scene-setting — that space is the platform's most expensive real estate

### Pacing & Structure
- Fast cuts and visual variety sustain attention through a 15-30 second ad far better than a single static shot, mirroring organic Reels editing conventions
- Native format mimicry: ads structured like organic Reels/UGC content (handheld feel, casual pacing, on-screen text captions) consistently outperform polished traditional commercial pacing for cold-audience prospecting
- CTA placement: reinforced both mid-video (for viewers who'll drop off) and at the end, not saved only for a final card

### Sound-Off Design
- Captions/on-screen text carrying the core message, since a large share of viewing happens muted
- Audio (trending sound, voiceover, music) as a secondary layer that helps native feel but shouldn't be load-bearing for comprehension

### UGC & Creator Briefing
- Briefing a creator or in-house talent with a hook line, key points, and desired CTA rather than a rigid script — natural delivery outperforms visibly-read ad copy
- Multiple takes/angles from one shoot to generate several distinct test variants efficiently

### Length & Format Fit
- Shorter (6-15s) for pure hook-driven awareness/retargeting reinforcement; longer (15-30s+) when there's a real story or objection-handling job to do (higher-consideration products)
- Length should be justified by content density, not padded to hit a round number

## Workflow
1. Confirm product, offer, awareness stage, and available production resources (existing footage vs. new shoot/creator briefing needed).
2. Write the hook first — first 1-3 seconds, before scripting the rest of the video.
3. Structure pacing with cuts/visual variety sustaining interest through the runtime.
4. Add captions/on-screen text carrying the full message independent of audio.
5. Place CTA reinforcement mid-video and at the close.
6. If briefing a creator, provide hook line + key points, not a rigid word-for-word script.
7. Produce multiple hook variants for testing rather than a single script.

## Outputs
- Video/Reels script or shot brief, hook-first
- Caption/on-screen text plan
- Creator briefing document, if applicable
- Multiple hook variants for testing

## Rules
- Never open a video ad with a slow brand intro or logo slate before the hook — this is the highest-cost mistake in Meta video creative.
- Always design for sound-off comprehension via captions.
- Match length to content density; don't pad runtime without a corresponding reason to hold attention that long.
