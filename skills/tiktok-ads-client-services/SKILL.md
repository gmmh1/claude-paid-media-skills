# TikTok Ads Client Services

## Purpose
Turn TikTok Ads expertise into a sellable service — pricing and scope that explicitly account for TikTok's unusually high creative-production dependency, which is the platform's biggest and most common source of client-agency friction.

## Trigger Conditions
- "How do I sell TikTok Ads management as a service?"
- "Price my TikTok Ads retainer"
- "Write a proposal for a prospective TikTok Ads client"
- "Onboard a new TikTok Ads client"

## Required Inputs
- Target client profile (DTC ecommerce, app, local business)
- Service scope offered (full management, creative production included or client-supplied, TikTok Shop management)
- Pricing model preference
- Client's realistic creative production capacity and appetite for creator partnerships

## Core Capabilities

### Pricing Models
- Flat fee, percentage of spend, or hybrid — same general tradeoffs as other platforms, with creative production even more clearly warranting separate, explicit pricing than on Meta, given how much more decisively it drives outcomes here
- Creator partnership budget as a distinct line item from both media spend and creative production fees, since it involves separate negotiation/payment with third parties

### Proposal Structure
- Setting explicit expectations upfront that TikTok success depends heavily on creative volume/velocity — a proposal that undersells this dependency sets up an unwinnable client relationship
- Referencing the platform's faster fatigue cycles when setting reporting/review cadence expectations

### Scope of Work Boundaries
- Creative production commitment stated explicitly (volume of new ad concepts/month, whether creator sourcing/management is included)
- TikTok Shop management, if applicable, as a distinctly scoped service line (catalog management, Shop-linked campaign optimization)
- Reporting cadence — recommend more frequent creative-performance check-ins than other platforms given faster fatigue cycles, even if strategic reviews stay monthly

### Client Onboarding
- Discovery questionnaire: business goals, target CPA/ROAS, existing creative assets, appetite/budget for creator partnerships, TikTok Shop status
- Access checklist: Business Center partner access, TikTok Shop access if applicable, brand asset handoff
- Expectation-setting on creative volume needs and fatigue cycles before the first report — this is the highest-friction education point specific to this platform

## Workflow
1. Clarify target client profile and service scope, explicitly addressing creative production ownership.
2. Recommend pricing with creative production and any creator partnership budget priced separately from media management.
3. Draft the proposal, foregrounding the creative-velocity dependency rather than treating it as a footnote.
4. Draft scope-of-work boundaries with explicit creative volume commitments and TikTok Shop scope if relevant.
5. Build the onboarding questionnaire and access checklist.
6. Set expectations on creative volume needs and fatigue cycles during onboarding.

## Outputs
- Pricing model recommendation with creative/creator budget priced separately
- Client-ready proposal draft foregrounding creative dependency
- Scope-of-work summary with explicit creative volume commitment
- Onboarding questionnaire and access checklist
- Expectation-setting note on fatigue cycles and required creative cadence

## Rules
- Always price and scope creative production explicitly and separately from media management — underscoping this is the single most common cause of TikTok client-agency conflict.
- Set fatigue-cycle expectations during onboarding, not reactively after a client asks why a previously working ad stopped performing.
- Never propose a TikTok engagement without a realistic creative volume commitment matched to the platform's faster iteration needs.

- **This is a generic starting template, not a competitive edge in itself** — it's published openly, so any competitor can read the same pricing logic. Treat it as a first draft to customize with real numbers, your actual positioning, and your track record before it goes to a client; your differentiation comes from execution quality and results, not from possessing this document.
