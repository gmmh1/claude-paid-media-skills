## Purpose
Build Account-Based Marketing campaigns targeting a defined list of named companies and the buying committee within them — the use case LinkedIn is uniquely positioned for given its company/title-level targeting precision.

## Trigger Conditions
- "Build an ABM campaign on LinkedIn"
- "Target our named account list"
- "Reach the buying committee at [target accounts]"
- "Account-based marketing strategy for LinkedIn"

## Required Inputs
- Target account list (named companies) — from CRM/sales team, or derived from ICP criteria if not yet built
- Buying committee roles to reach within each account (decision-makers, influencers, users, procurement/finance where relevant)
- Sales alignment status: is sales aware of and coordinated with this campaign (ABM works best as a marketing+sales joint motion, not marketing operating in isolation)
- Budget — ABM campaigns often run on smaller, more concentrated budgets than broad-ICP campaigns since the addressable audience is deliberately narrow

## Core Capabilities

### Target Account List Development
- Building or refining the account list from ICP criteria if the business doesn't already have one from sales/CRM
- Tiering accounts (e.g., Tier 1 highest-value/highest-fit for heavier investment, Tier 2/3 for lighter-touch coverage) rather than treating the whole list uniformly

### Buying Committee Mapping
- Identifying the roles typically involved in the purchase decision for this product/category: economic buyer, technical evaluator, end user, procurement — varies significantly by deal size and category
- Layering job function/seniority targeting on top of the company list so the campaign reaches the right people within target accounts, not just anyone at the company

### Campaign Structure for ABM
- Company List (Matched Audience) uploaded as the base targeting layer, combined with role-based professional targeting
- Personalization by tier/segment: messaging can and should differ for a Tier 1 account with named-account-level personalization potential vs. broader Tier 2/3 coverage using segment-level messaging
- Multi-touch sequencing: awareness/thought-leadership content to the full committee, followed by role-specific conversion offers (e.g., ROI calculator for economic buyer, technical deep-dive for evaluator)

### Sales Alignment
- Coordinating campaign timing and messaging with sales outreach to the same accounts — ABM's effectiveness compounds when marketing and sales touch the same accounts with consistent, complementary messaging rather than working in isolation
- Feeding campaign engagement signals (who at a target account engaged with what) back to sales as a prioritization signal, where CRM integration supports it

### Measurement
- Account-level engagement and reach metrics (accounts reached, engagement rate by account tier) as the primary ABM success metric, not just aggregate lead count — a smaller number of high-quality, right-committee-member engagements can outperform a larger generic lead count
- Pipeline influence/account progression as the ultimate measure, requiring coordination with sales/CRM data beyond what LinkedIn's own reporting shows

## Workflow
1. Confirm or help build the target account list, tiered by priority.
2. Map the buying committee roles relevant to this product/category.
3. Build campaign structure: company list + role targeting, tiered personalization.
4. Design multi-touch sequencing (awareness → role-specific conversion offer).
5. Coordinate timing/messaging with sales outreach to the same accounts.
6. Set up measurement around account-level engagement and, where possible, pipeline influence rather than raw lead count alone.

## Outputs
- Tiered target account list
- Buying committee role map
- Campaign structure (company list + role targeting, tiered personalization)
- Multi-touch sequencing plan
- Sales alignment recommendation
- Account-level measurement framework

## Rules
- Never run ABM as a marketing-only motion without at least flagging the value of sales coordination — isolated ABM underperforms its potential.
- Judge ABM success by account-level engagement/quality, not aggregate lead volume — a small number of right-committee-member engagements matters more than a large generic lead count.
- Tier the account list rather than treating it uniformly; concentrate personalization effort on highest-value accounts.
