## Purpose
Build Demand Gen campaigns — Google's visually-led, social-feed-style format spanning YouTube Shorts/in-stream, Discover, and Gmail — designed to create demand among audiences who aren't yet actively searching, using creative that performs like organic social content rather than traditional display ads.

## Trigger Conditions
- "Set up a Demand Gen campaign"
- "Run ads on YouTube Shorts / Discover / Gmail"
- "Build lookalike audiences for Demand Gen"
- "I want social-style ads through Google"

## Required Inputs
- Creative assets: vertical/short-form video (for Shorts), high-quality lifestyle/product images (for Discover feed placements), and standard image sizes for Gmail
- Conversion goal (Demand Gen supports awareness/consideration and conversion-optimized bidding)
- Audience signals: custom segments, customer match lists, or a seed audience for lookalike/similar-audience expansion
- Whether this is a standalone campaign or meant to feed a remarketing funnel downstream

## Core Capabilities

### Placement & Format Fit
- YouTube Shorts and in-feed: vertical, native-feeling short video performs dramatically better than repurposed traditional video ads — treat this as a distinct creative brief, not a resize job
- Discover feed: large, high-quality lifestyle imagery that blends with editorial/content feed aesthetics outperforms obvious product-shot ads
- Gmail: promotions-tab placement behaving like a hybrid of display and native email content

### Creative Strategy
- Demand Gen rewards native, scroll-stopping creative over polished traditional advertising — brief creative production accordingly (route to visual/video creation skills for asset production, this skill defines the strategic brief)
- Multiple creative variants per audience/theme, since Demand Gen's delivery optimizes across combinations similarly to PMax
- Carousel format for product-forward businesses wanting to show a range within one ad unit

### Audience Building
- Custom segments (interest/intent-based) as a starting signal
- Lookalike/similar-segment audiences built from Customer Match seed lists (coordinate with `google-ads-remarketing`) — seed list quality and size directly determine expansion audience quality
- Broad vs. narrow signal tradeoff: Demand Gen generally performs better with moderately broad signals than hyper-narrow targeting, given its demand-creation purpose

### Funnel Role
- Demand Gen sits mid-funnel: past pure awareness (Display/YouTube brand campaigns) but before high-intent Search — sequence it as a bridge that feeds remarketing pools for Search/Shopping to later convert
- A/B testing creative angle and format (Shorts-first vs. Discover-first emphasis) since placement performance varies significantly by business/category

## Workflow
1. Confirm available creative assets and flag any gaps (especially native vertical video, which is the highest-leverage asset type and most often missing).
2. Define the audience: custom segments and/or a Customer Match seed list for lookalike expansion.
3. Brief creative production for placement-native formats — not resized traditional ads.
4. Build multiple creative variants across the campaign's asset groups.
5. Set conversion goal and bidding, informed by how much conversion history exists.
6. Position the campaign in the funnel sequence — feeding remarketing pools rather than expecting it to convert at Search-level efficiency directly.

## Outputs
- Creative brief by placement (Shorts/in-feed, Discover, Gmail)
- Audience signal and lookalike/similar-segment plan
- Funnel positioning recommendation (what this campaign feeds into downstream)
- Asset gap flags, especially for native vertical video

## Rules
- Never brief Demand Gen creative as a straight resize of traditional Display/Search assets — placement-native creative is the core performance driver of this campaign type.
- Don't treat Demand Gen as a direct-response bottom-funnel replacement for Search; position it as demand-creation feeding remarketing and Search downstream.
- Flag when the available creative asset library is too thin (e.g., only static product shots, no video) to realistically perform well in Shorts/in-stream placements.
