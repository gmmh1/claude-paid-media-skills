## Purpose
Grow a YouTube channel's organic reach and retention — content strategy, discovery/SEO optimization, packaging (titles/thumbnails), and audience retention mechanics — as the free counterpart to paid YouTube ads, and the asset/audience base those ads eventually leverage.

## Trigger Conditions
- "Grow my YouTube channel"
- "YouTube SEO / why aren't my videos getting views"
- "Improve my titles and thumbnails"
- "Build a YouTube content calendar"

## Required Inputs
- Channel niche/topic and current subscriber base
- Recent video performance data if available (views, average view duration/retention curve, click-through rate on impressions)
- Content production capacity (frequency realistically sustainable)
- Whether the goal is subscriber growth, watch-time/ad revenue, or brand-building feeding a broader business (informs content strategy weighting)

## Core Capabilities

### Discovery & SEO
- Title and description keyword alignment with what viewers actually search/browse for, without resorting to misleading clickbait that tanks retention and triggers algorithmic demotion
- Tags as a minor, largely legacy signal — deprioritize relative to title/description/thumbnail/retention as ranking factors
- Closed captions/transcripts as both an accessibility requirement and a minor discovery signal

### Packaging (Title + Thumbnail)
- Thumbnail as the single highest-leverage click-through lever — high contrast, legible at small size, emotionally/visually distinct from the video's own recent thumbnails to avoid sameness fatigue
- Title-thumbnail pairing that creates a clear, honest curiosity gap — the click-through driver — without over-promising relative to what the video delivers, since the resulting retention drop punishes future recommendation
- A/B testing packaging (where available) rather than guessing

### Retention Mechanics
- Average view duration and audience retention curve as the dominant signal YouTube's algorithm weighs for recommendation — a video with strong CTR but a steep early drop-off underperforms a video with modest CTR and strong retention
- Hook structure in the first 15-30 seconds to prevent early drop-off (state the payoff, avoid slow intros/lengthy channel branding before value starts)
- Pacing and structure (chapters, visual variety, avoiding dead air) to sustain retention through the middle of longer videos

### Content Strategy
- Content pillars matched to both audience demand and business goal (a small set of recurring formats/topics outperforms scattered one-off ideas for building a recognizable channel identity)
- Series/format consistency that trains the audience on what to expect and return for
- Balancing search-driven "evergreen" content (steady long-tail views) against trend/timely content (spikes, faster but shorter-lived reach)
- Shorts as a discovery/reach funnel into long-form content, with its own distinct packaging and hook conventions (see `google-ads-demand-gen`'s Shorts creative principles for the paid-format equivalent)

### Community & Consistency
- Upload consistency/cadence as a trust and algorithmic-favor signal, more important than sporadic high-frequency bursts
- Community engagement (comments, community posts, end screens/cards driving session time) supporting session-watch-time, a metric YouTube's recommendation system also weighs

## Workflow
1. Review recent video performance (CTR and retention curve specifically) to diagnose whether the channel's issue is a discovery problem (low impressions/CTR) or a retention problem (people click but leave fast).
2. If discovery is the issue, focus on title/thumbnail packaging and topic-search alignment.
3. If retention is the issue, focus on hook structure, pacing, and honest title-thumbnail-content alignment.
4. Define 2-4 content pillars matched to audience demand and business goal, and a sustainable upload cadence.
5. Build a content calendar balancing evergreen and timely content within the defined pillars.
6. Recommend Shorts as a top-of-funnel discovery layer feeding long-form content, if not already in use.

## Outputs
- Discovery vs. retention diagnosis for underperforming content
- Title/thumbnail packaging recommendations
- Content pillar and cadence recommendation
- Content calendar structure
- Hook/retention structure guidance for upcoming videos

## Rules
- Never recommend clickbait packaging that overpromises relative to content — it produces a short-term CTR gain and a longer-term retention/recommendation penalty.
- Diagnose discovery problems and retention problems separately; they have different root causes and different fixes.
- Recommend a sustainable cadence over a burst-then-abandon posting pattern — consistency is itself a ranking-relevant signal.
